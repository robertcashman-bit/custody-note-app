          Authorities

          Standard authority forms for criminal defence work. Expand each to see proposed wording. Adapt as required for your firm and matter.

          Medical Authority

          Authorises release of medical records (GP, hospital, mental health) to your firm for the purpose of the client’s defence.

          Proposed wording

          MEDICAL AUTHORITY

          I, [Client full name], of [address], hereby authorise and request:

          1. My General Practitioner and any medical practice holding my records;

          2. Any hospital or NHS Trust that has treated me;

          3. Any mental health service or professional that has assessed or treated me;

          to release and disclose to [Firm name], my solicitors, all medical records, reports, notes and information relating to my physical and/or mental health that they hold, for the purpose of my legal proceedings and my defence.

          I understand that this information will be treated in confidence and used only for that purpose. I consent to copies of this authority being sent to the above in support of a request for disclosure.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Legal Aid Declaration

          Client declaration as to financial circumstances and consent for legal aid application (adapt for CRM2 / means assessment).

          Proposed wording

          LEGAL AID DECLARATION

          I, [Client full name], declare that the information I have given to [Firm name] about my income, capital and outgoings for the purpose of my application for criminal legal aid is true and complete to the best of my knowledge and belief.

          I understand that I must tell the Legal Aid Agency (or my solicitors) immediately if my financial circumstances change. I consent to my solicitors passing my details and this declaration to the Legal Aid Agency and to the use of my information for the assessment of my eligibility for legal aid.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Authority to Transfer Papers

          Client authorises transfer of the case file to another firm or solicitor (e.g. on change of solicitor or transfer of matter).

          Proposed wording

          AUTHORITY TO TRANSFER PAPERS

          I, [Client full name], hereby authorise [Current firm name] to send to [New firm / solicitor name and address] all papers, documents, correspondence and other material held in connection with my matter (including [brief case description, e.g. police station attendance / magistrates’ court matter]).

          I understand that my file will be transferred in its entirety and that my current solicitors will retain no copies unless required by law or professional rules. I consent to the disclosure of such information as is necessary to effect the transfer and to enable the new firm to act for me.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Authority to Obtain Documents

          General authority to request documents from police, CPS, court, previous solicitors or other third parties.

          Proposed wording

          AUTHORITY TO OBTAIN DOCUMENTS

          I, [Client full name], hereby authorise [Firm name], my solicitors, to request and receive from:

          • The police (including the investigating force and any custody/evidence holder);
          • The Crown Prosecution Service;
          • Any court or tribunal;
          • Any previous solicitors or legal representatives who have acted for me;

          all documents, statements, exhibits, unused material, disclosure, and any other papers or information relating to my matter/case. I consent to my solicitors providing a copy of this authority where necessary to support such requests.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Authority to Instruct Expert

          Client authorises the firm to instruct a named expert (e.g. medical, forensic, psychiatrist) and to disclose relevant papers.

          Proposed wording

          AUTHORITY TO INSTRUCT EXPERT

          I, [Client full name], authorise [Firm name], my solicitors, to instruct [Expert name / type, e.g. a consultant psychiatrist / forensic expert] to prepare a report and, if required, to give evidence in connection with my case.

          I consent to my solicitors disclosing to the expert such of my papers, medical records and other information as they consider necessary for the expert to carry out their instructions. I understand that the expert may need to examine me or discuss my case and I agree to cooperate. I consent to the report and any related correspondence being shared with the court, prosecution and legal aid authority as appropriate.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Consent to Disclosure (Third Parties)

          Client consents to disclosure of information to specified third parties (e.g. court, counsel, expert, family) where needed for the conduct of the case.

          Proposed wording

          CONSENT TO DISCLOSURE

          I, [Client full name], consent to [Firm name], my solicitors, disclosing such information and documents relating to my case as they consider necessary to:

          • The court and any tribunal;
          • Counsel and other advocates instructed on my behalf;
          • Experts instructed on my behalf;
          • [Other: e.g. a named family member / interpreter / intermediary] where relevant to my defence or to the conduct of my case.

          I understand that such disclosure will be limited to what is necessary and that confidentiality will be maintained so far as possible. This consent may be withdrawn in writing at any time.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          Authority to Act

          Client confirms instruction of the firm and authority to act (useful for court, LAA or third-party confirmation).

          Proposed wording

          AUTHORITY TO ACT

          I, [Client full name], confirm that I have instructed [Firm name] to act for me in connection with [brief description of matter]. I authorise them to conduct my case, to correspond and communicate with the court, prosecution, Legal Aid Agency and any other body or person as they consider necessary, and to sign documents on my behalf where I have given them specific authority to do so.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          DWP / Benefits Records Authority

          I consent to such records being disclosed to my solicitors. This request is made under the UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018 and the information will be used only for the purpose of my case.

          Signed: ……………………… Date: ………………

          How to Use Custody Note

          Custody Note is a complete digital attendance note and LAA billing tool for police station representatives. Everything is stored locally on your computer — no internet connection is required. This guide covers every feature in detail.

          Need help? Support forum · Contact us · FAQ

          Getting Started

          When you first launch the app, you will be asked for your DSCC PIN/Number. This is used in PDF headers and billing. You can skip this and set it later in Settings.

          Recommended first steps:

          1. Go to Settings (gear menu, top-right) and enter your Fee Earner Name. This auto-fills into conflict certificates and LAA declarations on every new record.
          2. Set your email address so exported PDFs can be sent to you for checking.
          3. Add the firms that instruct you via the Firms page. Star one as your default firm.
          4. Set a backup folder in Settings so your data is automatically backed up every 2 minutes (and hourly archives kept). Optionally set an off-site backup folder (e.g. OneDrive or Dropbox) so backups also copy to the cloud.
          5. Set a recovery password in Settings > Security & Encryption. This lets you recover your database if you move to a new computer.
          Licence Activation and Trial

          Custody Note requires a valid licence or trial to run. When you first install the app, you can start a 30-day free trial to try all features.

          Trial

          • On first launch, if no licence is entered, the app will offer a trial. Click Start trial to begin.
          • The trial lasts 30 days and includes all features, including automatic cloud backup to AWS.
          • You will see a reminder as the trial nears expiry. When expired, the app will show the licence overlay until you enter a valid licence key.

          Activating a subscription

          • After purchasing a subscription at custodynote.com, you will receive a licence key by email.
          • Enter your licence key in the activation overlay when prompted (e.g. when trial has expired or when opening the app without a valid licence).
          • The app validates your licence online. You need an internet connection for activation and periodic validation (the app has a grace period when offline).
          • If you see a "subscription" or "trial" message and you have paid, ensure you entered the exact licence key from your email. Keys are case-sensitive; enter them exactly as shown.

          Renewing or changing your subscription

          To renew, upgrade, or cancel, log in to your account at custodynote.com or use the link in your subscription confirmation email. The app will automatically recognise an active subscription when it next validates.

          Taskbar or desktop shortcut opens an old version?

          If you already have Custody Note installed, updates download automatically in the app. If the Custody Note icon on your taskbar or desktop launches an older version after you install an update, the shortcut is still pointing at the previous install. Fix: Unpin from the taskbar (right‑click the icon → Unpin from taskbar). Then open the Start Menu, search for Custody Note, right‑click it → Pin to taskbar. That pins the current installed version.

          Home Screen

          The home screen is your command centre. It shows a greeting, the current date and time, and action cards:

          • New Tel Advice — Start a telephone advice record (INVB claim). Use this for police station advice calls where you do not physically attend.
          • Quick Capture — Rapidly capture key case details when you first receive an instruction. Choose Custody or Voluntary mode; you can expand into a full form later.
          • Custody Attendance — Start a full police station attendance note when the client is in custody (under arrest). Includes PACE reviews, detention clock, and custody record.
          • Voluntary Attendance — Start a voluntary interview attendance note when the client attends voluntarily (not under arrest). Client is free to leave unless arrested. No PACE reviews or detention clock.

          Below the cards you will see Latest activity showing your most recent records (up to 10). Use View all to open the full list in All Records. A status strip confirms your internet and backup status.

          The search bar at the top of the header lets you search across all records by client name, UFN, station, custody number, or date.

          Quick Capture

          Quick Capture is designed for the moment you receive an instruction — while driving, on the phone, or in a rush. At the top, choose Attendance Type: Custody (client under arrest) or Voluntary (client attending voluntarily).

          It captures the essential details:

          • Client name, police station, offence, DSCC number
          • Instruction time, first contact time (LAA requirement)
          • Instructing firm, OIC details, custody number
          • Travel times (set off / arrived)

          You have two options when saving:

          • Save as Draft — Saves the quick capture data. You can open it later from All Records to continue.
          • Expand to Full Form — Immediately opens the full attendance form (custody or voluntary) with all your quick capture data pre-filled.
          New Attendance (Full Form)

          The main attendance form has 9 sections in the flow (1–9), navigated with Previous/Next or the Sections button. Consents & Retainer, Third Party Authority, Authorities, Communications Log, Supervisor Review, LAA Declaration, and Admin & Billing are not part of this flow — they open in a separate screen when you tap their card on section 1 or from Home.

          1. Case Reference & Arrival

          The first field is Attendance type — choose Custody (client under arrest) or Voluntary (client attending voluntarily). Changing this switches the form to the appropriate layout. Then: DSCC number, UFN, work type, instruction time, first contact with client, police station (typeahead search), instructing firm, OIC details. The police station field lets you type the first few letters and select from the LAA station list.

          2. Journey to Station

          Set-off time, arrival time, mileage. Used for travel and waiting time calculations.

          3. Custody Record

          Client personal details (name, DOB, address, nationality), NI number, benefits, passported benefit status (auto-detected from the benefit you select), employment, accommodation, marital status, risk assessment, PACE reviews, ethnicity and disability codes.

          Tip: Date of birth accepts flexible formats — type 15/03/1987, 15-3-87, or 15 mar 1987 and it will auto-parse.
          Tip: When you select a benefit type, the app automatically sets whether it is a passporting benefit (Universal Credit, Income Support, income-based JSA/ESA, Pension Credit Guarantee = Yes; all others = No).

          4. Offences

          Record up to four offences with details and statute. These are used to auto-fill charges if the client is charged (see Outcome section).

          5. Disclosure & Evidence

          Record what disclosure was received, PACE searches, forensic samples, and the name of the complainant/alleged victim.

          6. Consultation (Attend on Client)

          Record your consultation with the client: advice given, conflict check, instructions, welfare, checklist items.

          7. Interview

          Record one or more interviews with start/end times, advice given, and notes. Use + Add interview for further interviews.

          Note: These notes are not verbatim and should not be relied upon as if a transcript.

          8. Outcome

          Record the outcome decision (NFA, Charged, Bail, RUI, etc.). If the client is charged, the app offers to pre-fill the charge details from the offences in Section 4 — a confirmation box appears so you can accept or decline.

          The Court Name field has typeahead autocomplete — start typing and matching courts appear.

          9. Time Recording & Fees

          Record attendance, travel, waiting, and advice times (social/unsocial hours). The app calculates whether the case is an escape fee case automatically. Includes an Invoice Sent tracker that auto-stamps the date and time when you mark it as sent.

          Stand-alone options (separate screen)

          These open in a separate screen when you tap their card on section 1 or from Home; they are not in the main 1–9 flow.

          • Consents & Retainer — Client retainer / authority to act. Fields auto-fill from client details entered earlier.
          • Third Party Authority — Record authority from the client to contact third parties. Each entry captures name, relationship, contact details, what information is authorised to disclose, and outcome. Use + Add third party for multiple entries.
          • Authorities — Appointed Solicitor (reference, contact details), Medical Authorities (per provider), Other Professional Authority (social services, housing, education, etc.).
          • Communications Log — Log telephone calls, emails, and text messages. Each entry: type, direction, date, time, party, summary. Use + Add entry for each.
          • Supervisor Review — Only shown if enabled in Settings. For firms that require supervisor sign-off.
          • LAA Declaration — Declaration text, client and fee earner names (fee earner auto-fills from Settings), signatures (pads or full-screen canvas).
          • Admin & Billing — File number (ours) / invoice number, UFN, LAA account, MAAT ID.
          Voluntary Attendance

          Use Voluntary Attendance when the client attends voluntarily under PACE — not under arrest, free to leave unless arrested. The form has 7 sections tailored to voluntary interviews:

          1. Matter Setup — Date, firm, client, location, OIC/interviewer, instruction source, DSCC reference, UFN, allegation.
          2. Voluntary Status & Rights — Attending voluntarily? Free to leave explained? Caution given? Constable present?
          3. Welfare & Safeguards — Youth/vulnerable adult, appropriate adult, interpreter, fitness to be interviewed.
          4. Disclosure & Preparation — Disclosure summary, advice given, reasons, interview strategy.
          5. Attendance Record — Consultation and interview times, representations made. If the client is arrested during attendance, complete arrest time and conversion notes, then use Convert to custody workflow to create a new custody record with audit trail.
          6. Outcome & Billing — Outcome code (CN04–CN11), sufficient benefit, time recording.
          7. Client Aftercare — What was explained, next steps, follow-up tasks.

          The Billing readiness panel (near Finalise) shows warnings for missing UFN, outcome code, DSCC reference, or sufficient benefit note. A confidentiality reminder appears before exporting or emailing.

          Telephone Advice (INVB)

          The telephone advice form is a streamlined 4-section form for police station advice calls where you do not physically attend:

          1. Call Details — DSCC number, station, firm, OIC, instruction time, first contact time.
          2. Client & Advice — Client details, offence, disclosure, advice given, sufficient benefit test.
          3. Outcome — Outcome decision, time recording, fee calculation.
          4. Sign Off — LAA declaration, client/fee earner names. Signatures are optional (toggle on if the client is present or the firm requires them).

          This form meets all minimum LAA requirements for INVB (telephone advice) claims. The fields required for an LAA audit pass are highlighted.

          Navigating the Form
          • Previous / Next buttons — at the bottom of each section to move between sections.
          • Sections button — opens a popup listing all sections so you can jump directly to any one.
          • Context bar — the bar below the header always shows the client name, station, date, and current section for quick reference.
          • Progress bar — the coloured bar above the bottom buttons shows how far through the form you are.
          • Auto-save — the form auto-saves your work every few seconds. You will see "Saved" appear near the top of the form.

          All Records

          Access All Records from the gear menu or by clicking View all on the home screen.

          • Search — search by client name, UFN, station, custody number, or date.
          • Filter — filter by All, Drafts, or Finalised records; and by Attendance Type (All / Custody / Voluntary / Telephone).
          • Sort — sort by newest, oldest, client name A-Z, or station.
          • Compact view — toggle compact view for a denser list.

          Record Actions

          Each record in the list has action buttons:

          • Edit — Opens the record for editing. If the record is finalised, you will be asked to confirm; the status reverts to draft until you re-finalise.
          • Print PDF — Opens an in-app print preview of the full attendance note with a Print dialog.
          • Export PDF — Saves the attendance note as a PDF file to your Desktop.
          • Duplicate — Creates a copy of the record for a further visit. Client details are carried over; attendance-specific details are cleared.
          • New matter — Creates a new matter for the same client (same as duplicate but clearly a new matter).
          • Delete — Deletes the record. Finalised records are soft-deleted (can be recovered). You will be asked for a reason.

          Click anywhere on the record row (not a button) to open it in view/edit mode.

          Signatures

          Signature pads appear in the LAA Declaration, Consents, and Supervisor Review sections. You can sign directly in the inline canvas, but for touchscreens (tablets, laptops with touchscreens) use the Sign button:

          1. Press the blue Sign button next to the signature pad.
          2. A full-screen overlay opens with a large white canvas.
          3. Sign with your finger or stylus.
          4. Press Done to save the signature back to the form, or Cancel to discard.
          5. Use Clear to wipe the canvas and start again.

          The inline pad also has Clear and Undo buttons.

          Signature timestamps (date and time) are automatically recorded when you sign.

          • Edit a firm — click the Edit button on any firm row to amend its name, LAA account, contact details, phone, or email. Click Save to confirm.
          • Star a firm — click the star icon to set a firm as your default. The default firm is auto-selected on new records.
          • Remove a firm — click Remove to delete a firm from your list.

          When creating a new record, you can select from your saved firms or add a new firm inline.

          Backup

          The app backs up your attendance database automatically as encrypted files. In Settings > Backup you can set:

          • Backup folder — Where backups are saved (e.g. Desktop or a dedicated folder). A quick backup runs every 2 minutes when you’ve made changes, saving a single file that is overwritten each time. An hourly archive creates a new timestamped file every hour; the app keeps up to 48 of these and deletes older ones. From the Attendance List (Records), use Backup now to create an immediate backup.
          • Off-site backup folder (optional) — A second folder (e.g. inside OneDrive, Dropbox, or Google Drive). Whenever a backup runs, the same encrypted files are copied here so they sync to the cloud. This gives you a copy away from your computer in case of loss, theft, or hardware failure. Your data is not sent to any server you don’t control — you choose the folder and the sync is handled by your cloud provider.

          All backups are encrypted. Set a recovery password (Settings > Security) so you can open a backup on another computer if needed.

          Security & Encryption

          Your database is encrypted with AES-256-GCM. Set a recovery password so you can access your data on a new computer. Keep this password safe.

          Saving, Finalising & PDF Export

          Records have two statuses:

          • Draft — work in progress, can be edited freely.
          • Finalised — locked for editing. To make changes, use the Amend button in All Records, which reverts the record to draft.

          To finalise a record, press Save / Exit and choose Finalise. The app checks for missing required fields and warns you before finalising.

          To export a PDF, press Ctrl + E or use the Export PDF button. The PDF (including the LAA declaration) is saved to your Desktop as a single file. To print, use the Print Attendance Note button or press Ctrl + P for an in-app print preview with a Print dialog. Before export, print, or email, a confidentiality reminder appears — only share with authorised recipients and ensure client confidentiality (SRA standards).

          Official LAA forms (CRM1, CRM2, CRM3, Applicant Declaration) are available in Settings and from the LAA Forms button in the form header. Forms are pre-filled from the current record where possible.

          LAA billing codes

          The form uses standard LAA codes for billing and claims. Key fields: DSCC number (from the duty solicitor call centre), UFN (Unique File Number from the instructing firm), MAAT ID (client identifier on the Legal Aid Agency system), and LAA account (for case tracking). These appear in Admin & Billing and in the form header. Use them to match your exported PDFs and time records to LAA claims and firm file systems.

          Data Safety & Privacy
          • All data is stored locally on your computer in an encrypted SQLite database.
          • No data is sent to any server. The app works fully offline.
          • The database is encrypted with AES-256-GCM. The encryption key is protected by Windows Credential Store.
          • Automatic backups run every 2 minutes (quick) and every hour (archive). You can set an off-site backup folder (e.g. OneDrive) so the same encrypted files are copied there and sync to the cloud.
          • Set a recovery password in Settings to protect against data loss if your computer is lost or replaced.
          • Deleted finalised records are soft-deleted (not permanently removed) for audit trail purposes.
          Cloud Backup

          Cloud Backup is an optional add-on that automatically uploads your encrypted database to a secure UK data centre (AWS London).

          • Incorruptible: Backups are protected by AWS Object Lock — once uploaded, nobody (not even us) can delete or modify them. Ever.
          • Encrypted: Your data is encrypted on your computer before upload. We cannot read your backups.
          • Isolated: Only your licence key can access your backups. No other subscriber can see your data (SRA-compliant).
          • Restore: If your computer fails, install Custody Note on a new machine, enter your licence key, and restore from the cloud. You will need your recovery password.

          To subscribe, go to Settings > Cloud Backup or visit custodynote.com/pricing.

          ✓ Cloud Backup included: With an active licence, your database is automatically backed up to AWS (encrypted, UK data centre). Connect to the internet to sync and enable backups.
